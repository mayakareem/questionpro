# Brand Tracking

## Plain English Definition

Ongoing research that measures how your brand is performing over time by tracking awareness, perception, consideration, and preference among your target audience. It's like a regular health check for your brand, showing whether your marketing efforts are working and how you compare to competitors.

## Best Used When

- You want to measure the impact of marketing campaigns or rebrandings
- You need to track brand health metrics over time (quarterly or annually)
- You're monitoring competitive positioning in your market
- You want early warning signals of brand problems before they hit revenue
- You need to justify marketing spend with measurable brand lift
- You're entering new markets and want to track awareness growth
- You have ongoing brand-building efforts that need accountability

## Not Ideal When

- You're a very early-stage startup with no brand presence yet
- You need tactical campaign feedback (use post-campaign surveys instead)
- Your brand rarely changes and you just need one-time measurement
- Budget is very limited (tracking is recurring cost)
- Your market is tiny and sample sizes would be unreliable
- You can't commit to tracking consistently over time (one wave isn't useful)
- You need product-level insights more than brand-level

## Typical Business Questions It Answers

- "Is our brand awareness growing in our target market?"
- "How do customers perceive our brand compared to competitors?"
- "Did our rebranding campaign improve brand sentiment?"
- "Which competitor is winning mindshare in our category?"
- "Are we seen as innovative? Trustworthy? Expensive?"
- "What percent of our target market considers us when making a purchase?"
- "Is our brand associated with the attributes we're trying to own?"

## Sample / Audience Guidance

**Sample Size:**
- 300-500 per wave for stable tracking
- 800-1000+ if you need segment analysis
- Larger samples for low-awareness brands (more people to find aware respondents)

**Frequency:**
- **Quarterly:** For fast-moving markets or during active campaigns
- **Bi-annually:** Most common for steady-state monitoring
- **Annually:** Minimum frequency for meaningful trends

**Who to Target:**
- Your target market (not general population)
- Include both aware and unaware respondents to track awareness growth
- Consistent audience definition across waves (apples-to-apples comparison)

**Recruitment:**
- Use QuestionPro panels to reach market
- Match demographics to target buyer profile
- Keep methodology consistent wave-to-wave

## Core Design Elements

**Core Metrics to Track:**

1. **Awareness**
   - Unaided: "What brands come to mind for [category]?"
   - Aided: "Which of these brands have you heard of?"

2. **Consideration**
   - "Which brands would you consider when shopping for [category]?"

3. **Preference**
   - "Which brand do you prefer?"
   - "Which brand did you choose/buy?"

4. **Brand Attributes**
   - "Which brands are: innovative, trustworthy, expensive, high-quality, etc.?"
   - Track attributes most important to your positioning

5. **Net Promoter Score (NPS)**
   - "How likely are you to recommend Brand X?"
   - Track for your brand and competitors if possible

6. **Brand Funnel**
   - Aware → Familiar → Considered → Preferred → Purchased

**Survey Structure:**
- Category awareness and usage
- Brand awareness (unaided, then aided)
- Brand consideration set
- Brand attributes and perceptions
- Purchase behavior
- Demographics
- 10-15 minutes total

**Competitive Set:**
- Include 3-6 key competitors
- Rotate competitor order to avoid order bias
- Update competitive set if market changes

## Typical QuestionPro Workflow

1. **Design Tracker**
   - Define core metrics (awareness, consideration, attributes)
   - Build questionnaire in QuestionPro
   - Include your brand + top 3-5 competitors
   - Set attribute list aligned to your brand strategy

2. **Establish Baseline**
   - Run Wave 1 (400-500 target market respondents)
   - This is your benchmark
   - Analyze initial results

3. **Set Up Recurring Waves**
   - Schedule next wave (quarterly/bi-annual/annual)
   - Use SAME questionnaire each time (consistency is critical)
   - Deploy to same audience profile

4. **Collect Data**
   - QuestionPro panel reaches target audience
   - Monitor fielding to ensure quality
   - Close wave when quota reached

5. **Analyze Trends**
   - Use QuestionPro trend reports to compare waves
   - Track movement in awareness, consideration, preference
   - Monitor attribute shifts
   - Compare to competitive set

6. **Report & Act**
   - Export dashboards showing trends over time
   - Highlight wins and warning signs
   - Share with marketing and exec teams
   - Adjust strategy based on insights

## Expected Outputs

- **Awareness Trends:**
  - Unaided awareness: X% in Q1, Y% in Q2, Z% in Q3
  - Aided awareness growth/decline
  - Awareness by segment (e.g., enterprise vs SMB)

- **Brand Funnel Performance:**
  - % of market aware of your brand
  - % who consider it
  - % who prefer it
  - % who purchased
  - Conversion rates between funnel stages

- **Competitive Positioning:**
  - Your brand vs Competitor A, B, C on key metrics
  - Share of voice in awareness
  - Perception gaps

- **Attribute Tracking:**
  - % associating your brand with "innovative" over time
  - Shifts in perception (e.g., moving from "expensive" to "premium value")

- **NPS Trends:**
  - Promoter, passive, detractor % over time
  - NPS score trajectory

- **Segment Insights:**
  - How different audiences perceive you
  - Where you're winning vs losing by segment

## How To Interpret The Output

**Awareness Trends:**
- **Growing awareness:** Marketing is working, keep investing
- **Flat awareness:** Need more reach or frequency
- **Declining awareness:** Competitive pressure or reduced marketing

**Consideration:**
- High awareness but low consideration? You have a perception problem
- Growing consideration? Your messaging is resonating

**Preference:**
- High consideration but low preference? Competitors outshine you on key attributes
- Growing preference? You're winning

**Brand Attributes:**
- Are you owning the attributes you want?
- Shifts toward desired attributes = progress
- Unexpected attribute associations = messaging gaps

**Competitive Benchmarking:**
- Closing the gap with competitors? Winning
- Losing ground? Time to rethink strategy

**Segment Differences:**
- Strong in one segment, weak in another? Tailor messaging
- Uniform perception across segments? Consistent brand experience

**NPS:**
- Rising NPS = healthier brand
- Falling NPS = customer experience issues

## Risks / Common Mistakes

**Risk: Inconsistent methodology**
- Changing questions or audience wave-to-wave makes trends meaningless
- **Mitigation:** Lock down survey design, keep it consistent, resist tweaking

**Risk: Short-term thinking**
- One bad wave doesn't mean disaster; look for multi-wave trends
- **Mitigation:** Analyze 3+ waves before making big decisions

**Risk: Tracking vanity metrics**
- Measuring awareness but not consideration/purchase is incomplete
- **Mitigation:** Track full funnel, focus on metrics that tie to revenue

**Risk: Ignoring competitors**
- Tracking your brand in isolation misses market context
- **Mitigation:** Always include competitive benchmarking

**Risk: Sample quality issues**
- Inconsistent panels or respondent quality skews trends
- **Mitigation:** Use same panel source, set quality standards, exclude bad responses

**Risk: No action on insights**
- Tracking for tracking's sake without adjusting strategy
- **Mitigation:** Tie metrics to decisions, set KPIs, review with leadership

**Risk: Overreacting to noise**
- Small fluctuations aren't meaningful (e.g., 32% to 34% awareness)
- **Mitigation:** Look for statistically significant changes, focus on patterns

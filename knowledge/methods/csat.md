# Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)

## Plain English Definition

A direct measure of how satisfied customers are with a specific product, service, or interaction. Typically uses a 1-5 or 1-7 scale ("How satisfied were you with...?"). CSAT is calculated as the percentage of respondents who selected the top satisfaction ratings (typically 4-5 on a 5-point scale). It's the most common and intuitive CX metric.

## Best Used When

- You need to measure satisfaction at a specific touchpoint (purchase, support call, onboarding)
- You want fast, actionable feedback on a recent interaction
- You need to track service quality across channels, agents, or locations
- You're measuring the impact of a specific change or improvement
- You want a metric that everyone in the organization intuitively understands
- You need to trigger immediate service recovery for dissatisfied customers
- You're benchmarking across locations, teams, or time periods

## Not Ideal When

- You need to predict long-term loyalty or churn (use NPS)
- You want to understand process friction or effort (use CES)
- You're measuring overall brand health (use brand tracking)
- You need deep diagnostic insight on complex experiences (use CX driver analysis)
- You want to understand feature preferences or trade-offs (use conjoint or MaxDiff)
- You're measuring something customers haven't directly experienced

## Typical Business Questions It Answers

- "How satisfied are customers with our support experience?"
- "Did our new onboarding process improve customer satisfaction?"
- "Which locations or agents are delivering the best customer experience?"
- "Are customers satisfied with our product after the recent update?"
- "What's driving dissatisfaction at checkout?"
- "How does satisfaction compare across channels (phone vs chat vs email)?"

## Budget & Timeline Reality Check

### Post-Interaction CSAT (Continuous)

**Cost:** $2-4K setup, minimal ongoing
**Timeline:** 1 week to set up, continuous thereafter
**Sample:** Census (all customers post-interaction)
**What you get:** Real-time satisfaction pulse, agent/location benchmarking

### Periodic CSAT Study (One-Time or Quarterly)

**Cost:** $5-8K per wave
**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks per wave
**Sample:** 200-400 respondents per segment
**What you get:** Satisfaction baseline, trend tracking, segment comparison

### Multi-Touchpoint CSAT Program

**Cost:** $10-20K setup + $3-5K quarterly
**Timeline:** 3-4 weeks initial setup, continuous thereafter
**Sample:** Variable by touchpoint volume
**What you get:** End-to-end satisfaction mapping, gap identification, prioritization

## What Good Looks Like

- **Excellent:** CSAT > 90% (top-2 box on 5-point scale)
- **Good:** CSAT 80-90%
- **Average:** CSAT 70-80%
- **Concerning:** CSAT < 70%

## Common Pitfalls

- Asking too late after the interaction (survey within 24-48 hours for accuracy)
- Surveying on every interaction (fatigue - sample or rotate)
- Confusing CSAT with NPS (CSAT = transaction, NPS = relationship)
- Not acting on low scores (collecting data without closing the loop)
- Using only the aggregate score (segment analysis reveals hidden issues)
- Ignoring the middle scores (4s and 3s are your conversion opportunity)

## Combines Well With

- **NPS** - CSAT for touchpoints, NPS for overall loyalty
- **CES** - CSAT for satisfaction, CES for effort/friction
- **Text Analytics** - Open-ended CSAT responses mined for themes
- **CX Driver Analysis** - CSAT as outcome, drivers explain what moves it
- **Mystery Shopping** - CSAT from customers, mystery shopping from trained evaluators

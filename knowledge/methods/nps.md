# Net Promoter Score (NPS)

## Plain English Definition

A standardized loyalty metric that asks customers one simple question: "How likely are you to recommend [company/product] to a friend or colleague?" on a 0-10 scale. Respondents are classified as Promoters (9-10), Passives (7-8), or Detractors (0-6). Your NPS = % Promoters minus % Detractors, giving you a score from -100 to +100.

## Best Used When

- You need a single, trackable metric for customer loyalty over time
- You want to benchmark against industry competitors
- You need an early warning system for churn risk
- You want to segment customers by loyalty level for targeted action
- Leadership needs a simple, universal metric they can rally around
- You want to trigger follow-up actions based on score (e.g., call every Detractor)
- You're running a relationship program (not just transactional satisfaction)

## Not Ideal When

- You need to understand WHY customers feel the way they do (NPS alone doesn't explain drivers)
- You're measuring a single transaction or touchpoint (use CSAT instead)
- Your business is B2B with very few customers (small sample = unreliable NPS)
- You need to measure effort or ease of experience (use CES instead)
- You're in a market where "recommend" doesn't apply (regulated industries, monopolies)
- You want detailed diagnostic insight on specific product areas

## Typical Business Questions It Answers

- "How loyal are our customers compared to last quarter?"
- "Are we losing ground to competitors on customer loyalty?"
- "Which customer segments are most at risk of churning?"
- "What's the impact of our recent product changes on loyalty?"
- "Which regions or business units have the strongest customer relationships?"
- "Is our loyalty improving or declining over time?"

## Budget & Timeline Reality Check

### Transactional NPS (Post-Interaction)

**Cost:** $3-5K setup, minimal ongoing
**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks to set up, continuous thereafter
**Sample:** All customers post-interaction (census approach)
**What you get:** Touchpoint-level loyalty pulse, fast feedback loop

### Relationship NPS (Periodic Survey)

**Cost:** $5-10K per wave
**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks per wave, quarterly recommended
**Sample:** 300-500 per segment for statistical reliability
**What you get:** Strategic loyalty tracking, competitive benchmarking, segment analysis

### Enterprise NPS Program

**Cost:** $15-30K setup + $5-10K per quarterly wave
**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks initial setup, quarterly thereafter
**Sample:** 500-1,000+ across segments, regions, products
**What you get:** Full closed-loop system with alerts, dashboards, action workflows

## What Good Looks Like

- **Excellent:** NPS > +50 (world-class loyalty)
- **Good:** NPS +30 to +50 (strong, competitive)
- **Average:** NPS 0 to +30 (room for improvement)
- **Concerning:** NPS < 0 (more detractors than promoters)

Industry benchmarks vary significantly - a +30 in telecom is excellent, while +30 in luxury retail is average.

## Common Pitfalls

- Running NPS without follow-up questions (you get a number but no insight)
- Surveying too frequently (survey fatigue depresses scores)
- Not closing the loop with Detractors (defeats the purpose)
- Comparing across industries without context (benchmarks vary wildly)
- Using NPS as the ONLY CX metric (it's a lagging indicator - pair with CSAT and CES)
- Gaming scores by only surveying happy customers

## Combines Well With

- **CSAT** - NPS for loyalty, CSAT for specific touchpoints
- **CES** - NPS for loyalty, CES for process friction
- **CX Driver Analysis** - NPS as outcome metric, driver analysis to find what moves it
- **Exploratory Interviews** - Qual follow-up with Detractors and Promoters to understand "why"
- **Segmentation** - Different segments have different NPS drivers

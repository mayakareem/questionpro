# Customer Effort Score (CES)

## Plain English Definition

Measures how easy or difficult it was for a customer to complete a specific task or resolve an issue. Uses a 1-7 scale ("To what extent do you agree: [Company] made it easy for me to handle my issue"). CES is one of the strongest predictors of customer loyalty - research shows reducing effort has a bigger impact on loyalty than delighting customers.

## Best Used When

- You want to identify friction points in customer journeys
- You're optimizing self-service channels (website, app, knowledge base)
- You need to measure support experience quality (was the issue easy to resolve?)
- You're redesigning processes and need before/after measurement
- You want a metric that directly predicts repurchase behavior
- You need to find where customers are getting stuck or giving up
- You're comparing channels for ease of use (phone vs chat vs self-service)

## Not Ideal When

- You're measuring overall satisfaction or emotional experience (use CSAT)
- You need a strategic loyalty metric (use NPS)
- The interaction doesn't have a clear "task" component
- You're measuring brand perception or awareness (use brand tracking)
- You want to understand feature preferences (use MaxDiff or conjoint)
- The customer journey is too complex to isolate a single task

## Typical Business Questions It Answers

- "How easy is it for customers to resolve support issues?"
- "Which processes create the most friction for customers?"
- "Is our self-service portal actually reducing customer effort?"
- "How does effort compare across support channels?"
- "Where should we invest to reduce customer friction?"
- "Did our process redesign actually make things easier?"

## Budget & Timeline Reality Check

### Post-Interaction CES (Continuous)

**Cost:** $2-4K setup, minimal ongoing
**Timeline:** 1 week to set up, continuous thereafter
**Sample:** Census (all customers post-interaction)
**What you get:** Real-time effort tracking, channel comparison, friction hotspots

### CES Journey Study (One-Time)

**Cost:** $8-15K
**Timeline:** 3-4 weeks
**Sample:** 300-500 respondents across key journeys
**What you get:** End-to-end effort mapping, priority friction points, improvement roadmap

### Ongoing CES Program

**Cost:** $10-20K setup + $3-5K quarterly
**Timeline:** 4 weeks initial, continuous thereafter
**Sample:** Variable by interaction volume
**What you get:** Longitudinal effort tracking, impact measurement, competitive benchmarking

## How It Works in QuestionPro

### Setup
1. Create survey using CES question format (1-7 agreement scale)
2. Specify the task/interaction being measured
3. Add open-ended follow-up ("What made this difficult?" or "What would have made this easier?")
4. Configure triggers post-interaction (email/SMS within 1-2 hours for support, 24 hours for processes)
5. Set up channel-specific surveys (different CES for phone, chat, web, app)

### Analysis
- Real-time CES dashboard with effort trends
- High-effort scenario identification (which tasks score worst)
- Channel comparison (where is effort highest?)
- Text analytics on open-ended responses for root cause
- Correlation with resolution time, contact reason, and agent

### Action
- Automated alerts for high-effort interactions
- Root cause analysis for top friction points
- Process improvement prioritization based on effort scores
- Before/after measurement for redesign initiatives

## What Good Looks Like

- **Excellent:** CES > 6.0 (out of 7) - most customers find it easy
- **Good:** CES 5.0-6.0
- **Average:** CES 4.0-5.0
- **Concerning:** CES < 4.0 - significant friction present

## Common Pitfalls

- Measuring CES on interactions that don't have a clear task (irrelevant)
- Waiting too long to survey (effort perception fades quickly - survey within hours)
- Not combining with CSAT/NPS (CES alone doesn't capture emotional experience)
- Ignoring the qualitative follow-up (the "why" behind effort scores is where the gold is)
- Averaging across all interactions (segment by task type, channel, complexity)
- Not tracking effort over time (one-time measurement misses trends)

## Combines Well With

- **CSAT** - CES for effort, CSAT for satisfaction (effort and satisfaction don't always correlate)
- **NPS** - CES for operational improvement, NPS for strategic loyalty tracking
- **CX Driver Analysis** - CES as outcome, driver analysis to find what creates effort
- **Mystery Shopping** - CES from real customers, mystery shopping to test specific processes
- **Exploratory Interviews** - Deep dive with high-effort customers to understand barriers

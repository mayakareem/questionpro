# QuestionPro Outputs & Deliverables

## Overview

This document describes what outputs you'll actually see when you run research on QuestionPro. For each methodology, we explain the specific deliverables, formats, and how to use them for decision-making.

---

## Standard Survey Outputs

### What You Get

**1. Real-Time Dashboard**
- **Response count**: Total responses and completion rate
- **Summary statistics**: Mean, median, mode for rating questions
- **Response distribution**: Charts showing answer frequencies
- **Filters**: Segment by demographics or responses

**2. Visualizations**
- **Bar charts**: For multiple choice questions
- **Pie charts**: For single-select questions
- **Line graphs**: For tracking over time
- **Heat maps**: For matrix questions
- **Word clouds**: For open-ended responses

**3. Cross-Tabulation (Crosstabs)**
- Compare segments (e.g., enterprise vs SMB responses)
- Filter by any variable
- Statistical significance testing available

**4. Text Analytics**
- Sentiment analysis (positive, neutral, negative %)
- Theme extraction (top topics mentioned)
- Word frequency
- Categorized verbatim responses

**5. Export Formats**
- **Excel**: Raw data, charts, summary tables
- **SPSS**: For advanced statistical analysis
- **CSV**: For custom analysis or BI tools
- **PDF**: Presentation-ready reports
- **PowerPoint**: Charts and tables ready to present

### How to Use These Outputs

**For Decision-Making:**
- Look for patterns: What do 60%+ say? That's a signal.
- Compare segments: Do enterprise and SMB customers want different things?
- Read open-ends: Numbers tell you "what," verbatims tell you "why"

**For Stakeholder Communication:**
- Export PDF report with key charts
- Pull compelling quotes from verbatims
- Show cross-tabs by segment if relevant

## NPS Outputs

### What You Get

**1. NPS Score**
- Single number from -100 to +100
- Calculation: % Promoters - % Detractors

**2. Distribution Breakdown**
- **Promoters** (9-10): % who gave 9 or 10
- **Passives** (7-8): % who gave 7 or 8
- **Detractors** (0-6): % who gave 0-6

**3. Trend Chart** (if recurring)
- NPS score over time (wave-by-wave)
- Shows whether you're improving or declining

**4. Segment Comparison**
- NPS by customer type (enterprise vs SMB)
- NPS by product usage level
- NPS by geography, industry, etc.

**5. Driver Analysis** (if included)
- What factors most strongly predict NPS
- Importance-performance matrix

**6. Verbatim Feedback**
- Open-ended "why" responses
- Categorized by promoter/passive/detractor
- Themes extracted via text analytics

### How to Interpret

**NPS Benchmarks:**
- **>50**: Excellent, world-class
- **30-50**: Good, healthy brand
- **10-30**: Room for improvement
- **<10**: Major issues, churn risk

**Action Items:**
- **Promoters**: Ask for referrals, testimonials, case studies
- **Passives**: Find out what would make them promoters
- **Detractors**: Immediate outreach (closed-loop feedback), identify patterns

## CSAT / CES Outputs

### What You Get

**1. CSAT Score**
- Average rating (e.g., 4.2 out of 5 or 8.1 out of 10)
- Response distribution (how many 1s, 2s, 3s, etc.)

**2. Top 2 Box Score**
- % who gave top 2 ratings (e.g., 4 or 5 on a 5-point scale)
- Industry-standard metric for satisfaction

**3. CES Score**
- Average effort rating (lower = better)
- % high-effort responses (typically 5-7 on a 7-point scale)

**4. Trend Over Time**
- Score by week, month, or wave
- Identify if satisfaction is improving or declining

**5. Touchpoint Breakdown**
- CSAT/CES by specific touchpoint (onboarding, support, billing)
- Identify which parts of experience need work

**6. Verbatim Comments**
- Why customers gave that score
- Themed by satisfaction level (high vs low)

### How to Use

**CSAT Benchmarks:**
- **>80% Top 2 Box**: Strong satisfaction
- **60-80%**: Acceptable
- **<60%**: Needs improvement

**CES Benchmarks:**
- **<3 on 7-point scale**: Low effort (good)
- **3-5**: Moderate effort
- **>5**: High effort (fix this)

**Action Items:**
- Track CSAT/CES by touchpoint to prioritize fixes
- Monitor trends to see if improvements are working
- Address high-effort scenarios immediately

## Van Westendorp PSM Outputs

### What You Get

**1. Price Sensitivity Chart**
- Four crossing lines showing:
  - Too cheap
  - Cheap (bargain)
  - Expensive
  - Too expensive

**2. Optimal Price Point (OPP)**
- Price where "too expensive" and "too cheap" lines cross
- Recommended starting price

**3. Indifference Price Point (IPP)**
- Price where "expensive" and "cheap" lines cross
- Where equal numbers think it's expensive vs cheap

**4. Range of Acceptable Prices**
- Lower bound: Where "too cheap" and "expensive" cross
- Upper bound: Where "too expensive" and "cheap" cross
- Your pricing flexibility zone

**5. Demand Curve** (approximate)
- Estimated % who'd buy at each price
- Visual representation of price sensitivity

### How to Interpret

**Optimal Price Point:**
- Start here unless you have strategic reasons to price higher/lower

**Acceptable Range:**
- Don't price outside this range
- Wide range = you have pricing power
- Narrow range = market has strong price expectations

**Decision Framework:**
- **Revenue maximization**: Price at OPP or slightly above
- **Market share focus**: Price below OPP
- **Premium positioning**: Price at high end of acceptable range

## MaxDiff Outputs

### What You Get

**1. Ranked Priority List**
- Items sorted from most to least important
- Example:
  1. Security (Score: 85)
  2. Ease of use (Score: 72)
  3. Speed (Score: 58)
  ...
  12. Custom branding (Score: 15)

**2. Relative Importance Scores**
- Each item gets a 0-100 score
- Scores sum to 100 across all items
- Shows magnitude of differences

**3. Statistical Significance**
- Confidence intervals for each score
- Shows which differences are meaningful vs noise

**4. Segment Comparison**
- Priority rankings by segment (e.g., enterprise vs SMB)
- Identify if different groups have different priorities

**5. Visual Chart**
- Bar chart showing relative importance
- Easy to see gaps between items

### How to Use

**Priority Tiers:**
- **Must-haves** (Score >60): Top priorities, build these first
- **Nice-to-haves** (Score 40-60): Important but not critical
- **Lower priority** (Score <40): Can defer or skip

**Decision-Making:**
- **Product roadmap**: Build top 3-5 features first
- **Marketing messaging**: Lead with top benefits
- **Sales enablement**: Emphasize top attributes in pitches

**Segment Strategy:**
- If enterprise prioritizes X but SMB prioritizes Y → need different messaging or tiers

## Conjoint Analysis Outputs

### What You Get

**1. Part-Worth Utilities**
- Numerical value for each feature level
- Example:
  - 128GB storage: -1.5
  - 256GB storage: +0.2
  - 512GB storage: +2.3
- Higher = more preferred

**2. Feature Importance Scores**
- % importance of each attribute (sums to 100%)
- Example:
  - Price: 35%
  - Storage: 30%
  - Speed: 20%
  - Camera: 15%

**3. Willingness to Pay**
- Dollar value of each feature
- Example: "Customers will pay $180 more for 512GB vs 256GB"

**4. Price Elasticity Curve**
- Demand at different price points
- Shows optimal price for revenue maximization

**5. Market Simulator**
- Input any product configuration
- Get predicted preference share
- Example: "Your product at $999 with premium features: 38% share"

**6. Segment Analysis**
- Part-worths and importance by segment
- Example: Enterprise values security (45%) vs SMB values ease (50%)

### How to Interpret

**Feature Importance:**
- Attributes >30% drive decisions (focus here)
- Attributes <10% may not be worth investing in

**Willingness to Pay:**
- If feature adds $X value, you can charge up to $X more
- ROI check: Does feature cost less to build than customers will pay?

**Market Simulation:**
- Test scenarios: "Premium features + $999" vs "Fewer features + $799"
- Choose configuration that optimizes for your goal (revenue, share, profit)

**Decision Framework:**
- **Product configuration**: Include high-utility features, drop low-utility ones
- **Pricing**: Price at demand curve peak for max revenue
- **Tiering**: Use segment differences to create good/better/best tiers

## Brand Tracking Outputs

### What You Get

**1. Awareness Metrics**
- **Unaided awareness**: % who mention your brand unprompted
- **Aided awareness**: % who recognize your brand from a list
- Tracked over time (wave-by-wave)

**2. Brand Funnel**
- Aware → Familiar → Considered → Preferred → Purchased
- Conversion rates at each stage
- Compared to competitors

**3. Competitive Benchmarking**
- Your brand vs Competitor A, B, C on all metrics
- Share of awareness, consideration, preference

**4. Attribute Associations**
- % who associate your brand with each attribute
- Example: "45% say we're innovative, 60% say we're expensive"
- Tracked over time to see perception shifts

**5. NPS by Brand**
- If you can survey users of each brand
- Comparison of loyalty across competitive set

**6. Trend Charts**
- All metrics plotted over time (quarterly or annually)
- Shows whether brand health is improving or declining

### How to Interpret

**Awareness Trends:**
- Growing awareness + flat consideration = perception problem
- Growing awareness + consideration = marketing working

**Brand Funnel:**
- Big drop-off from aware to considered? Messaging issue
- High consideration but low preference? Competitors winning on key attributes

**Attribute Shifts:**
- Moving toward desired attributes? Progress
- Unexpected associations? Messaging gaps

**Competitive Position:**
- Gaining share of awareness/consideration? Winning
- Losing ground? Time to adjust strategy

## CX Driver Analysis Outputs

### What You Get

**1. Driver Ranking**
- Attributes ranked by impact on outcome (satisfaction, NPS, retention)
- Example:
  1. Support quality: 30% importance
  2. Onboarding: 20% importance
  3. Product features: 18% importance
  ...

**2. Importance-Performance Matrix**
- 2x2 grid showing:
  - **Fix first**: High importance, low performance (priority)
  - **Maintain**: High importance, high performance (strengths)
  - **Low priority**: Low importance, low performance
  - **Potential over-investment**: Low importance, high performance

**3. Predicted Impact**
- "If we improve support quality by 1 point, NPS increases by 5 points"
- ROI estimation for improvements

**4. Current Performance Scores**
- How satisfied customers are with each attribute (1-10)
- Identifies gaps

**5. Segment Differences**
- How drivers differ by segment (enterprise vs SMB, new vs tenured)

**6. Verbatim Themes**
- What customers say about each driver (from open-ended questions)

### How to Interpret

**High Impact Drivers (>15% importance):**
- These are your leverage points—improve these first

**Performance Gaps:**
- High importance + low performance = urgent priority
- Example: Support is critical (30%) but rated poorly (5/10) → fix support

**Quick Wins:**
- Items in "fix first" quadrant of matrix
- High impact, currently underperforming

**Strengths:**
- High importance + high performance → keep doing well, don't divert resources

**ROI Prioritization:**
- Focus on drivers where a 1-point improvement yields biggest NPS/CSAT lift

## Text Analytics Outputs

### What You Get

**1. Sentiment Breakdown**
- % Positive, Neutral, Negative
- Overall and by segment

**2. Top Themes**
- Most frequently mentioned topics
- Example: "Pricing" (mentioned 120 times), "Ease of use" (mentioned 95 times)

**3. Word Cloud**
- Visual representation of most common words
- Bigger words = mentioned more frequently

**4. Categorized Verbatims**
- Responses grouped by theme
- Example: All "pricing" comments together

**5. Sentiment by Theme**
- Which topics are positive vs negative
- Example: "Support" has 80% positive sentiment, "Onboarding" has 40% negative

**6. Quote Library**
- Searchable database of responses
- Filter by sentiment, theme, or demographic

### How to Use

**Identify Patterns:**
- What themes appear most? Those are top-of-mind issues
- What has negative sentiment? Those are pain points

**Contextualize Quantitative Data:**
- NPS is 30 (low)—text analytics tells you why ("poor support response times")

**Stakeholder Communication:**
- Share powerful verbatim quotes
- More impactful than numbers alone

**Action Items:**
- Negative sentiment themes → prioritize fixing these
- Positive sentiment themes → highlight in marketing

## Summary: What QuestionPro Gives You

QuestionPro delivers **data + insights + visualizations** in formats ready for decision-making:

- **Numbers**: Scores, rankings, percentages
- **Visuals**: Charts, graphs, dashboards
- **Stories**: Verbatim quotes and themes
- **Comparisons**: Segments, competitors, time trends
- **Predictions**: Market simulations, driver impact

The platform automates most analysis, so you get insights quickly without needing deep statistical expertise. Outputs are designed to be shared with stakeholders—from raw data for analysts to presentation-ready reports for executives.

**Key Principle:** QuestionPro outputs should answer "What should we do?" not just "What did we learn?"
